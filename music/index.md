# My musical journey

_June 2026_

I've always felt bound to music in a special way. The way it carries emotions was always a mystery, as if a magician were casting a spell on me.

How come a song makes me want to cry? How come it makes me feel nostalgic or hopeful?\
There ought to be a trick, I thought, and I want to unveil it, to understand how it works.

This eagerness to fathom the underlying principles of this magic has waxed and waned, fading away as I was swamped by distractions only to resurge when I stumbled upon some strong arcane artifacts.

Masterpieces of virtuosos such as Hisaishi Joe, Howard Shore or even Keiichi Okabe, were somehow strongly resonating with my inner self, pulling me toward them.

This time I chose not to resist the attraction, I chose not to let the curiosity dim anew. Today is the day I stop fiddling with basic sleights of hand, I'll turn myself into a conjurer.

## My background

I've played piano for a year when I was around 8 years old, and I've been playing the bass (and the guitar) for about 20 years now.

20 years of paganistic usage of the forbidden tablatures, keeping me away from the sacred scores.

**Rules**

Chord Progression: | Am | F | C | G | Am | F | C | G | (8 measures, 4/4 time, 80 bpm, A minor)

Range: C4 → E5

Steps:

- Harmonic Anchor: Place a chord tone on beats 1 and 3 of every measure (no jumps larger than a fourth).
- Connect: Link these notes using stepwise motion (passing tones on weak beats, using the A minor scale).
- Shape: Check the arc; the climax must occur in measures 5-6, not earlier.
- Critical Listening: Identify what sounds flat or generic. Test a rhythmic variation to break the academic stiffness.
