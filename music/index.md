I've always felt bound to music in a special way. The way it carries emotions was always a mystery, as if a magician were casting a spell on me.

How come a song makes me want to cry? How come it makes me feel nostalgic or hopeful?\
There ought to be a trick, I thought, and I want to unveil it, to understand how it works.

This eagerness to fathom the underlying principles of this magic has waxed and waned, fading away as I was swamped by distractions only to resurge when I stumbled upon some strong arcane artifacts.

Masterpieces of virtuosos such as Hisaishi Joe, Howard Shore or even Keiichi Okabe, were somehow strongly resonating with my inner self, pulling me toward them.

This time I chose not to resist the attraction, I chose not to let the curiosity dim anew. Today is the day I stop fiddling with basic sleights of hand, I'll turn myself into a conjurer.

## My background

I've played piano for a year when I was around 8 years old, and I've been playing the bass (and the guitar) for about 20 years now.

20 years of paganistic usage of the forbidden tablatures, keeping me away from the sacred scores.

#### Constrained Melody

##### Exercice 1.1a

###### Rules

Chord Progression:

| Am | F | C | G | Am | F | C | G |

8 measures, 4/4 time, 80 bpm, A minor

Range: C4 → E5

Steps:

- Harmonic Anchor: Place a chord tone on beats 1 and 3 of every measure (no jumps larger than a fourth).
- Connect: Link these notes using stepwise motion (passing tones on weak beats, using the A minor scale).
- Shape: Check the arc; the climax must occur in measures 5-6, not earlier.
- Critical Listening: Identify what sounds flat or generic. Test a rhythmic variation to break the academic stiffness.

##### Exercise 1.1c

###### Rules

Steps:

- Same method as Exercise 1.1a: Skeleton → Fill-in.
- Constraint: Start on E (the fifth of Am).
- Prohibition: Do not play A on any strong beat (beat 1 or 3) before measure 8.
- Purpose: Delay the tonic to create tension that only resolves at the end.

##### Exercise 1.2a

###### Rules

Chord Progression:

| Dm7 | Gm7 | Em7b5 | A7 | Dm7 | Bbmaj7 | Gm7 | A7/Dm |

8 measures, 4/4 time, A minor (or related mode)

Structure: Antecedent / Consequent

Steps:

- Skeleton: Place one chord tone on beats 1 and 3. No leaps larger than a fourth.
- Antecedent (measures 1-4): End on an unstable scale degree to create tension.
- Consequent (measures 5-6): Reuse the exact rhythm from measures 1-2.
- Measures 7-8: Resolve the melody toward the tonic on a strong beat.
- Climax: Ensure the melodic peak occurs within the consequent phrase.

##### Exercise 1.2b

###### Rules

Chord Progression:

| Dm7 | Gm7 | Em7b5 | A7 | Dm7 | Bbmaj7 | Gm7 | A7/Dm |

8 measures, 4/4 time, A minor

Structure: Antecedent / Consequent

Steps:

- Skeleton: Place chord tones on beats 1 and 3.
- Antecedent (measures 1-4): Fill with leaps (thirds, fourths, fifths). End on an unstable scale degree.
- Consequent (measures 5-6): Reuse the rhythm from measures 1-2 but alter specific notes.
- Consequent (measures 7-8): Fill using stepwise motion (conjunct movement).
- Resolution: Resolve to E (the tonic of C major/A minor relative context or specific target note).
- Climax: Ensure the melodic peak occurs within the consequent phrase.

##### Exercise 1.3

###### Rules

Chord Progression:

Choose your own progression

8 measures, 4/4 time, any key

Steps:

- Write 8 measures of blocked chords using your chosen progression.
- Identify the highest note of each chord to form a raw melody line.
- Adjust inversions so the top line draws an interesting arc.
- Extract the line and dress it as a melody (rhythm, added notes, rests).
- Preserve anchor notes.
- Put the chords back, listen, and adjust.
